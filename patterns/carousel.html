Carousel

  • Use cases
  • Guidelines
  • Research
  • Related items

A carousel, otherwise known as a slideshow or image slider, allows multiple pieces of content to occupy high value real estate at the top of a landing page. From, December 2019 to February 2021, CFPB’s homepage featured a carousel.

Carousels offer poor usability and are considered an anti-pattern. Their use is discouraged.

Standard carousel

The standard carousel organism is comprised of a set of blocks of content that can be navigated through sequentially. Each content item includes a title, description, link and image.

Unlike other carousel patterns, CFPB’s carousel does not automatically progress through its items. A user must either click the previous/next arrow or select one of the thumbnail previews at the bottom of the component.

Guidelines

Content guidelines

  • Carousel item titles should be as succinct as possible, 45 characters maximum (including spaces). Sentence case, unless proper noun.
  • Item descriptions should be 140 characters maximum (including spaces).
  • Item link text has a 30 character maximum limit (including spaces). Lead with a verb, and be specific.

Research

There is a plethora of research discouraging the use of carousels on websites:

  • Image Carousels and Sliders? Don’t Use Them.
